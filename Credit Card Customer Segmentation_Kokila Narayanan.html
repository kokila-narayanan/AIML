# Finding optimal number of K

# Importing cdist to find distance between two points, in this case centroids
from scipy.spatial.distance import cdist
cluster = range(1,10)
AvgDist = [] #Mean distortion is initialized
totRows = ccd_s.shape[0] 

for k in cluster:
    model = KMeans(n_clusters = k)
    model.fit(ccd_s)
    prediction = model.predict(ccd_s)
    # Distance to all clusters
    dist_all = cdist(ccd_s,model.cluster_centers_,'euclidean')
    # Distance to own cluster
    dist_own = np.min(dist_all,axis=1)
    # Calculate Average Distortion and append to array
    AvgDist.append(sum(dist_own)/totRows)
    
    
# Elbow plot - K vs Average Distortions
plt.plot(cluster,AvgDist,'bx-')
plt.xlabel('K')
plt.ylabel('Average Distortion')
plt.title('Elbow plot')

Choosing a 'linkage' method to use for prediction:¶

Between the 4 linkage methods we have used, 'Average' has the highest cophenetic coefficient closer to 1 followed by 'Centroid', 'Complete' and 'Ward'.

But as noted above, the clustering of 'Complete' seems to have even variance across the various clusters. I will use both 'Average' and 'Complete' to cluster the data and see which has the highest silhouette score.

Insights from clusters comparison:¶
  • There are 3 segments of customers
  • Average Credit Limit and Total Credit cards seem to influence the clusters mainly
  • Both KMeans and Hierarchical clustering have segmented the customers in the same manner. ###### Cluster 1: <KMeans: 1, HC: 2> Customers with Low credit limit and lower number of credit cards ###### Cluster 2: <KMeans: 0, HC: 3> Customers with Medium credit limit and medium number of credit cards ###### Cluster 3: <KMeans: 2, HC: 1> Customers with Highest credit limit and most number of credit cards ######
  • There is 1 customer who moved from Cluster 1 in KMeans to Cluster 2 in Hierarchical
  • Both clustering techniques point that Cluster 1 customers prefer calling the customer service more
  • Cluster 2 customers prefer in-person visit to the bank
  • Cluster 3 customers tend to use online customer service more

Response to Key Questions:¶

  1. How many different segments of customers are there? ######
    There are 3 different segments ######
  2. How are these segments different from each other? ######
    These segments differ by Average Credit Limit and Total number of credit cards mainly. Based on this, we can see a clear correlation to whether the customers in that segment prefer online services, phone services or in-person bank visits. ######
  3. What are your recommendations to the bank on how to better market to and service these customers? ######
    i. Among the targeted customers, 59% customers are in Cluster 2 with medium credit limit and medium number of credit cards. The mean credit card numbers for this segment is around 6 which overlaps with the Cluster 3 range. Hence, the marketing team should focus on upselling to this segment. ######
    ii. Similar to above, 34% customers are in Cluster 1 with lower credit limit and lower number of credit cards. The marketing team can focus on upselling to this segment to slowly bring them to the lower range of Cluster 2. ######
    iii. In terms of customer service, majority of customers are using online or phone customer service. Delivery team should focus resources more in these two areas to provide quick and efficient service. ######
    iv. For new marketing campaigns and offers, use online portal as well as phone marketing more than bank visits ###### v. To improve customer service, ensure efficient online portal services, online customer service chats and effective phone customer service.
